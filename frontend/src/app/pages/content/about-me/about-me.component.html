Ctrl + Shift + I (How I started programming)

Like many others, that was my first experience with code. Growing up in a foreign country and not knowing how to speak the language well, I often found myself trying to make friends by making them laugh. It wasn’t long before I found out how to “hack” into wikipedia to play tricks on my friends. I still fondly remember those times in primary school where my friends and I would constantly try to one-up each other with our new “hacking” skills.

Unfortunately, after our school built a new playground, our days of changing static html documents during lunch time came to an abrupt end.

But it was not until I started my computer science degree that I wrote my first “Hello World” program. And ever since then, I have fallen in love with programming. I love the idea of building applications to solve problems, and the scalability of digital products fascinated me. I could not wait for my degree to teach me the skills I needed, so I learnt web-development largely on my own, specifically focusing on the MEAN stack. Since then I have broadened my skills by learning about other relevant topics such as algorithms and data structures.

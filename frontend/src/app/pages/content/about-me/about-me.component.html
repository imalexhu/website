Ctrl + Shift + I (How I started programming)

Like many others, that was my first experience with code. Growing up in a foreign country and not knowing how to speak the language well, I often found myself trying to make friends by making them laugh. It wasn’t long before I found out how to “hack” into wikipedia and change it to something inappropriate. I still fondly remember those times in primary school where my friends and I would constantly try to one-up each other with our new “hacking” skills.

Unfortunately, after our school got more funding and a real playground was built, our times of changing static html documents came to an abrupt end.

But it was not until the start of my computer science degree, did I ever write my first “Hello World” program. And ever since then, I have fallen in love with programming. I love the idea of building applications to solve people’s problems, and the scalability of a digital product fascinated me. I could not wait for my degree to teach me the skills I needed, so I learnt web-development largely on my own, specifically focusing on the MEAN stack.
